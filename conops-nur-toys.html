Why a ConOps Document?

A Concept of Operations bridges the gap between a business owner's day-to-day reality and a developer's technical roadmap. Before any system is built, this document answers: who does what, when, and how — and what changes once the new process goes live.

Diagnosis

Operational Pain Points Identified

Surfaced through stakeholder interviews and on-site workflow shadowing at the SME's consignment counters.

Inventory Mismatch

Physical stock counts at partner consignment counters frequently drift from handwritten records, with no real-time way to catch the gap.

Settlement Delays

Profit-split payouts to partner stores stall while both sides manually cross-check paper ledgers, often weeks after the sale occurred.

Shrinkage Blind Spots

Damaged or missing stock between supply points and distributors goes undetected until monthly audits finally surface the loss.

The Proposed System Model

From Paper Ledgers to Verified Digital Records

The CONOPS redesigns the settlement cycle so both the SME owner and consignment partners operate from the same real-time, cloud-synced source of truth.

Current State — Manual Process
1

Paper Sales Logging

Each transaction at a consignment counter is written by hand into a notebook.

2

End-of-Day Manual Tally

Staff cross-check the physical stock against the handwritten ledger at closing.

3

Monthly Reconciliation

Owner and partner stores compare separate notebooks weeks after the transactions happened.

4

Discrepancies Surface Late

Shrinkage and damaged-stock gaps are only noticed once the reconciliation begins.

5

Delayed, Disputed Settlement

Profit-split payouts stall pending manual dispute resolution between both parties.

Proposed State — ConOps Digital System
1

Digital Sales Logging

Every transaction is entered into a cloud-synced system the moment it happens.

2

Real-Time Stock Sync

Inventory counts update instantly across both the owner's and partner's dashboards.

3

Dual Sign-Off Verification

Owner and consignment partner confirm stock counts together, creating shared accountability.

4

Unalterable Audit Log

Every entry is timestamped and locked, removing room for record tampering or "missing pages".

5

Automated Settlement

Profit-split payouts trigger automatically once verified data is confirmed by both sides.
